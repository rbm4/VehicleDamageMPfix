title=No Extra Car Damage MP B42.16 bugfix
description=[h1]Vehicle Damage MP Fix[/h1]
description=Fixes a vanilla multiplayer bug where vehicle damage from collisions is multiplied by the number of online players.
description=
description=[h2]The Problem[/h2]
description=In multiplayer, every connected client independently runs its own Bullet physics simulation. When a vehicle hits a zombie, player, or object, [b]each client[/b] that has loaded that vehicle sends a damage command ([i]damageFromHitChr[/i] / [i]crash[/i]) to the server - even though the collision only happened once.
description=
description=The vanilla server handler blindly applies damage from [b]all[/b] of these reports without any authorization or deduplication. This means:
description=
description=[list]
description=[*] With 2 players nearby: the vehicle takes [b]2x[/b] damage
description=[*] With 5 players nearby: the vehicle takes [b]5x[/b] damage
description=[*] With 10 players nearby: the vehicle takes [b]10x[/b] damage
description=[/list]
description=
description=The more players on the server (or near the vehicle), the faster vehicles are destroyed. This makes cars feel like paper in populated servers and significantly impacts the multiplayer experience.
description=
description=[h2]The Fix[/h2]
description=This mod overrides the server-side [i]VehicleCommands[/i] handler and introduces a simple authorization check:
description=
description=[list]
description=[*] [b]Driven vehicles:[/b] Only the [b]driver's client[/b] is allowed to apply collision damage. Reports from all other clients are rejected.
description=[*] [b]Driverless vehicles:[/b] Left as vanilla behavior (no change).
description=[/list]
description=
description=The result: a driven vehicle takes [b]exactly 1x[/b] damage per collision, regardless of how many players are online - just like in singleplayer.
description=
description=[h2]Why Not Fix Driverless Vehicles Too?[/h2]
description=An earlier version of this mod used a server-side [i]OnTick[/i] hook to track timing and deduplicate damage reports for driverless vehicles. This was removed because hooking [i]OnTick[/i] runs code [b]every single server frame[/b], which is an aggressive amount of processing for an edge case.
description=
description=Driverless vehicles have no authoritative client - there is no "owner" to trust, so the server has no reliable way to pick which client's damage report is the correct one without per-frame tracking infrastructure. Since driverless vehicle collisions are a rare scenario (e.g., a parked car hit by another vehicle or a zombie), the tradeoff was made to keep the mod lightweight and only fix the main case: [b]driven vehicles[/b], which account for the vast majority of collision damage in normal gameplay.
description=
description=[h2]Details[/h2]
description=[list]
description=[*] [b]Server-side only[/b] - no client-side files are modified
description=[*] [b]Zero performance overhead[/b] - no OnTick hooks, no per-frame logic; just a single check when a damage command arrives
description=[*] [b]Safe[/b] - all other vehicle commands are left identical to vanilla
description=[*] [b]Compatible[/b] - works with any mod that doesn't fully replace VehicleCommands.lua
description=[/list]
description=
description=[h2]Installation[/h2]
description=Subscribe and enable the mod on your server. No client-side installation needed - any player can join without having the mod.
description=
description=[h2]Compatibility[/h2]
description=[list]
description=[*] Build 42+
description=[*] Multiplayer only (does nothing in singleplayer)
description=[*] Safe to add or remove mid-save
description=[/list]
